Course Project – Smart Health & Infrastructure

City: PRITHVI LOK – Applying Advanced Algorithms to Real Urban Challenges

Region: 150 sq km
Budget: ₹20,000 crore
Theme: Health & Infrastructure Planning
Focus: Graphs, Trees & Advanced DS

This course project models Prithvi Lok, a planned smart city spread across 150 sq km with a health-first infrastructure mission. Each business case below connects a realistic urban problem with specific algorithms and data structures (Heap, Segment Tree, Hash Tables, Trie, DFS/BFS, Fenwick Tree, BST, MST, Bellman–Ford, Red-Black Tree, Dijkstra, Floyd) to achieve measurable impact under the ₹20,000 crore budget. Every case is mapped to SDG themes like good health, sustainable cities and reduced inequalities.

CASE 01

Emergency Travel-Time Routing for City Hospitals

Prithvi Lok – trauma, cardiac, and maternity emergency coverage

Problem
Ambulances must reach 95% of citizens within 15 minutes despite peak-hour congestion.
Algorithms
Dijkstra’s Algorithm for fastest path on weighted road graph.
Data Structures
Graph adjacency lists + Heap (priority queue) for efficient relaxations.
Purpose
Compute real-time least-time ambulance routes from each station to all hospitals.
Efficiency
O((V + E) log V), supports frequent updates as traffic changes.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3 (Good Health): median response time reduced by ~40%, improving survival rates.

CASE 02

Ward Connectivity Mapping for Health Camps

Prithvi Lok – outreach to slums, peri-urban belts & hill clusters

Problem
Health department needs to know which wards are poorly connected to PHCs for mobile camps.
Algorithms
BFS + DFS to explore ward-to-ward road graph and detect weakly served clusters.
Data Structures
Graph adjacency list; visitation arrays; simple queues & stacks.
Purpose
Identify connected components & longest reach paths for planning weekly health camps.
Efficiency
O(V + E) for full city scan; scalable to future expansions.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities): coverage of remote wards increased from 72% → 96%.

CASE 03

Affordable Health Shuttle Network with Subsidies

Prithvi Lok – integrating bus passes, e-rickshaws & metro feeder routes

Problem
Low-income patients need the cheapest route to hospitals when some routes give subsidies.
Algorithms
Bellman–Ford to handle negative-cost edges representing travel subsidies.
Data Structures
Edge list representation; distance arrays updated relaxatively.
Purpose
Compute minimum net travel cost from slum clusters to each hospital under subsidy rules.
Efficiency
Runs in O(V · E); executed overnight for planning fare tables.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 1 & 3: average out-of-pocket transport cost reduced by ~30% for target households.

CASE 04

All-Pairs Travel Time Between ICUs and PHCs

Prithvi Lok – referral planning during seasonal outbreaks

Problem
During disease surges, PHCs must know the best ICU to refer to with minimal travel delay.
Algorithms
Floyd–Warshall for all-pairs shortest paths between key health nodes.
Data Structures
Distance matrix (NxN) for hospitals & PHCs, updated by dynamic programming.
Purpose
Pre-compute referral matrix for instant decisions during ICU bed shortages.
Efficiency
O(N³) for N key nodes; feasible since N is small (< 60).
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3: better referral choices reduce critical transfer delays by 20–25%.

CASE 05

Fiber Network for Hospital & PHC Telemedicine

Prithvi Lok – connecting 2 super-specialty, 6 general & 18 PHCs

Problem
Government must connect all health facilities with fiber at minimal trenching cost.
Algorithms
Minimum Spanning Tree (Kruskal’s) on weighted graph of fiber routes.
Data Structures
Edge list sorted by cost + Disjoint Set (Union–Find).
Purpose
Choose subset of fiber links that connects all facilities with minimum construction cost.
Efficiency
O(E log E); much faster than trying all combinations.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 9 & 3: tele-consult access for rural belts; ~18% saving in planned fiber budget.

CASE 06

Live Bed & Ventilator Availability Across Wards

Prithvi Lok – ICU, HDU and general bed monitoring dashboard

Problem
Control room must answer “beds free in zone 3–7?” in milliseconds as updates stream in.
Algorithms
Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree) – extendable to Segment Trees.
Data Structures
Fenwick Tree over bed counts per facility index.
Purpose
Support fast range-sum queries and point updates for “beds available per corridor / zone”.
Efficiency
O(log N) for both update and query; works for thousands of beds.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3: smoother load balancing between hospitals; fewer patients turned away at gates.

CASE 07

Prithvi Lok Smart Health Card Lookup

Fast retrieval for 32 lakh resident health IDs

Problem
OPD desks must verify citizen health IDs in under 0.5 seconds during registration.
Algorithms
Hashing with collision resolution (chaining / open addressing).
Data Structures
Hash Table keyed by Health ID ⟶ minimal profile info & pointers to full records.
Purpose
Instant verification and fraud detection at registration counters across the city.
Efficiency
Average lookup in O(1); supports millions of IDs.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 16: reduced identity fraud; faster queues improve patient satisfaction.

CASE 08

Auto-Complete Search for Clinics & Streets

Citizen health app – “type 3 letters, see nearest facilities”

Problem
Patients struggle to spell long local names; app must suggest clinics as they type.
Algorithms
Trie (Prefix Tree) for prefix-based auto-complete suggestions.
Data Structures
Trie storing clinic names + tags (24x7, blood bank, maternity, etc.).
Purpose
Retrieve top matching facilities in real time as the user types 2–3 characters.
Efficiency
Lookup in O(L) where L is query length; robust for thousands of names.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3 & 11: easier discovery of nearby care, especially for new migrants.

CASE 09

Specialist Doctor Directory for Referral Desk

Prithvi Lok – search by specialization, experience & rating

Problem
Referral desks must quickly find suitable doctors when patients have specific needs.
Algorithms
Balanced BST operations (insert, search, range query).
Data Structures
BST keyed by <specialization, rating, years>; can be extended to AVL / Red-Black Tree.
Purpose
Quickly suggest top 3–5 doctors satisfying specialization and minimum rating filters.
Efficiency
Average O(log N) search; supports dynamic doctor onboarding.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3: better match between patient condition and specialist expertise.

CASE 10

Fair Queue for Critical Ward Upgrades

Prithvi Lok – allocating ₹2,000 crore for PHC upgrades

Problem
City must prioritize PHCs for renovation based on risk score, load & deprivation index.
Algorithms
Red-Black Tree to maintain a balanced priority ordering of PHCs.
Data Structures
Red-Black Tree keyed by composite priority score, supporting inserts & deletions.
Purpose
Always pick the next highest-priority PHC for upgrade under the limited budget.
Efficiency
All operations in O(log N) while keeping the tree balanced.
SDGs & Outcome
SDG 3 & 11: ensures deprived localities are not starved of infrastructure upgrades.
